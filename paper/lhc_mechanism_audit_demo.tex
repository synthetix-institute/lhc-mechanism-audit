\begin{abstract}
Scientific controversies are often represented as provenance graphs: who made a claim,
which document contains it, and who agrees or disagrees. This is useful for attribution,
but it does not test whether the physical mechanism exists. We demonstrate a second
graph on the LHC microscopic-black-hole example. The public audit reads static
equation witnesses and separates a provenance graph from a mechanism graph. In
\SelectedSources{} selected arXiv sources, the strict gate found \EquationWitnesses{}
equation witnesses, \UsableMechanisms{} usable formula-clean mechanism nodes,
\CaseMechanisms{} LHC-black-hole case-relevant nodes, and \EvidenceGradeMechanisms{}
evidence-grade case nodes. It found \DirectSafetyMechanisms{} formula-clean direct
LHC-safety mechanisms, \ThresholdHooks{} collider-threshold or event-selection hook,
and \AstroAnalogues{} astrophysical black-hole analogues. The result is not a final
safety review. It is a demonstration that the serious audit layer is mechanism
translation from adjacent physics, not claim counting.
\end{abstract}

\section{The Problem}

A provenance graph can tell us which paper asserted safety, danger, or a criticism.
It cannot by itself test the branch structure of the physical argument. For the LHC
black-hole case, the relevant scientific question is not whether a sentence says
``safe'' or ``dangerous''. The question is whether the chain of physical mechanisms
can be made explicit:
\[
\mathrm{production}\rightarrow
\mathrm{evaporation\ or\ stability}\rightarrow
\mathrm{capture/stopping}\rightarrow
\mathrm{accretion/growth}\rightarrow
\mathrm{astrophysical\ bound}.
\]
This is why the demo builds two graphs from the same papers.

\section{Two Graphs in the Same Archive}

\textbf{Provenance graph.} Nodes are sources and claims. Edges record that a
source makes a claim or belongs to a claim family. This graph is inspectable but
does not decide whether a claimed mechanism closes.

\textbf{Mechanism graph.} Nodes are equation witnesses and local formula
transitions. Edges record source-local route transitions and cross-source route
analogues. A node is evidence-grade only if the local formula window contains
both the black-hole case and a mechanism category. Source-level words alone are
not enough.

\section{Mechanism Gates}

The public demo uses six route labels as a readable interface to equation
transformations:
\[
\begin{array}{ll}
\text{transport/flow} & \partial_t q + \nabla\cdot J = S,\\
\text{constraint/closure} & C(q,J,\lambda)=0,\\
\text{spectral/operator} & Lq=\lambda q,\\
\text{boundary/weak form} & \int_\Omega \phi Lq=\int_\Omega \phi f,\quad Bq=b,\\
\text{commutator/residual} & [A,B]=AB-BA,\\
\text{discrete protocol} & x_{n+1}=\Phi(x_n,u_n).
\end{array}
\]
These are not final physical claims. They are audit gates that decide which
equations are usable as mechanism receipts.

\section{Mechanism Translation to the LHC Branch}

The LHC branch needs a production or event-selection condition, for example
\[
\sqrt{s} > M_D ,
\]
followed by a branch decision. If evaporation dominates, one expects a mass-loss
condition schematically of the form
\[
\frac{dM}{dt} < 0,\qquad \tau_{\rm evap}\ll \tau_{\rm capture}.
\]
If a stable object is assumed, the relevant mechanism changes to capture and
growth:
\[
\frac{dM}{dt} = \rho\,\sigma(M)\,v .
\]
Astrophysical survival bounds then enter as falsifiers. A dangerous growth
branch would have to be compatible with compact-object observations; schematically,
\[
t_{\rm grow} < t_{\rm WD}
\]
would conflict with the observed survival of white dwarfs if the same mechanism
were already realized in nature. The public graph found many adjacent
astrophysical mechanisms of this type, but no direct formula-clean LHC-safety
mechanism under the strict gate.

\section{Why This Beats a Provenance-Only Graph}

A provenance-only graph can identify papers, authors, dates and claim families.
It is useful for scrutiny of attribution. It fails when the important question is
mechanistic: whether the physical branch closes. In this demo, provenance can
organize disagreement about LHC safety, but the mechanism graph shows why the
scientific evidence is indirect. The direct safety branch is empty under the
strict gate. The adjacent astrophysical branch is populated. This distinction is
not visible from a claim map alone.

\section{Boundary}

This report does not claim to resolve the LHC safety question. It demonstrates a
public, inspectable audit object built from static outputs. The Hyperion core is
not included in this repository. The public files contain derived graph
artifacts, run summaries, figures and report text only.
