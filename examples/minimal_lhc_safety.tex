\section{Minimal fixture for LHC black-hole safety audit}
Hypothetical production of a microscopic black hole at a collider is relevant
only in a branch where the production threshold is reached,
\[
  \sqrt{s} > M_D .
\]
If Hawking evaporation is active, the mass loss branch can be represented as
\[
  {dM\over dt} = - {a\over M^2}, \qquad \tau_{\rm evap} \propto M^3 .
\]
If the object is assumed stable, the safety question moves to capture and
accretion:
\[
  {dM\over dt} = \rho\,\sigma(M)\,v .
\]
The astrophysical bound compares the same stable-object branch with cosmic-ray
production in dense stars.  If dangerous objects are produced and captured, the
growth time must be shorter than the lifetime of the object:
\[
  t_{\rm grow} < t_{\rm WD}
\]
would contradict the observed survival of white dwarfs and neutron stars.
